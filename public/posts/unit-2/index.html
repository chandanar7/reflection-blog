   My Experience with Understanding Information-

Going into this lab, I honestly felt a mix of confusion and slight stress. The first task was installing tools like Hugo and Git, and I wasn’t completely confident about doing it on my own. Even though it was supposed to be a simple setup, I kept second guessing whether I was doing things correctly. At the same time, once I got through it (with a bit of help and checking),it honestly felt satisfying. It made me realize that sometimes the fear of starting is worse than the actual task. When I started working with The Information: A History, a Theory, a Flood, I initially thought it would be just another reading task. But it turned out to be more thought provoking content than I expected. What I found slightly difficult was focusing on what the author is saying instead of adding my own opinions. I’m used to reacting to what I read, so sticking to pure summarizing took alot of effort. One thing that stayed with me was how information has evolved from simple communication to something that can actually be measured and analyzed. I had never really thought about information in that way before.

      Writing was not easy as it looks-

Writing the blurb and summaries was harder than I expected. Making something short but still interesting (especially the blurb) took more time than writing longer paragraphs. I kept rewriting it because it either felt too plain or too long. It was more of a group effort which had to be disturbuted among ourseleves it took alot of co-operation. Also, working on Outline made me more conscious about how I write, because others would read it. It wasn’t just “write and submit” it felt like my work had to actually make sense to someone else. That pressure was a bit stressful but also pushed me to do better.

       What Actually Stayed With Me-

What really stood out to me from this lab was how information is not just “content” we scroll through daily. It has structure, history, and impact. That idea honestly changed how I look at things like social me did, news, and even messages. I also found it interesting and a bit overwhelming that information overload isn’t just a modern issue it has been building up over time. It made me reflect on how easily I consume information without questioning it. Another thing I realized about myself is that I tend to overthink tasks, especially when they involve something new. But discussing with my group and seeing others approach the same task differently helped me feel less stuck.

          Final Thoughts-

Overall, this lab was more than just completing steps it made me think, reflect, and also step out of my comfort zone a little. Even though I felt confused and unsure at the beginning, by the end I felt like I had a better understanding not just of the topic, but also of how I approach learning. It showed me that learning isn’t always smooth or perfect, but the process itself is what actually matters.

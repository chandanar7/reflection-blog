   My Experience with Understanding Information-

Going into this lab, I honestly felt a mix of confusion and slight stress. The first task was installing tools like Hugo and Git, and I wasn’t completely confident about doing it on my own. Even though it was supposed to be a simple setup, I kept second guessing whether I was doing things correctly. At the same time, once I got through it (with a bit of help and checking),it honestly felt satisfying. It made me realize that sometimes the fear of starting is worse than the actual task. When I started working with The Information: A History, a Theory, a Flood, I initially thought it would be just another reading task. But it turned out to be more thought provoking content than I expected. What I found slightly difficult was focusing on what the author is saying instead of adding my own opinions. I’m used to reacting to what I read, so sticking to pure summarizing took alot of effort. One thing that stayed with me was how information has evolved from simple communication to something that can actually be measured and analyzed.
